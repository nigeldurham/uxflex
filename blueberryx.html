sign up redesign

Our Team

As the sole designer on this project, I directed redesigns for BlueberryX's online membership sign up flow. Working remotely with engineers and executive stakeholders, I iteratively modified existing UX architecture for the online user experience.

Product Ideation

With the goal of increasing user sign ups, I analyzed BlueberryX's existing flow for pain points along the user journey. Verifying and tackling existing user pain points through an iteratvie design process showed that conversion was driven through an increase in meaningful content and a simplification of CTAs.

Problem Space

Users traversed a rather winding route through the originial product - I sought to simplify the users' journey, reducing page traversals by consolidating product information and highlighting meaningful content aligning with CTAs.

Wireframing Solutions

Simplifying the existing landing page, we wireframed a solution facilitating smoother flow tile-to-tile. Incorporating new visual assets and simplifying existing copy, users' gaze was directed to CTAs that facilitated movement through their journey.

Product Prototyping

Minimizing the amount of visual, copy, and journey-based clutter, we reduced the flow complexity to direct users to their end goal with simplicity and ease.

Usability Testing

Users were tested in-person or online. Brand-unfamiliar users noted that they felt the original design had done little to bring them to sign up for a membership, commenting that assets did not meaningfully highlight product features or answer basic questions about product usage or function.

Our updated design was seen as highlighting important key features, making the product easier to understand or learn more about.

Brand-familiar users, alternavitely, noted that they felt both designs were in line with the expected product experience.

Feature Prioritization

Providing more succinct, organized information about product incentives was engaging for new, brand-unfamiliar users. Highly detailed specifications on product functionality and features did not increase membership sign ups for new users.

Final Prototype

A/B testing showed that our design changes significantly increased conversion for BlueberryX's product, most notably for brand-unfamiliar users. We saw an 87% increase in sign ups overall, more than half of those which are represented by brand-unfamiliar users.

Our Product

Increasing Blueberryx's sign ups by a significant margin, our simplified sign up flow greatly increased brand awarenss in the online space, specifically engaging new users with little to no experience with the brand.

This engagement brought broader platform gains including expanded opportunities for research and new product feedback.
